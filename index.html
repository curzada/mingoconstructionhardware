Limestone

Limestone is a sedimentary rock primarily composed of calcium carbonate (CaCO3), usually in the form of calcite or aragonite. It forms from the accumulation of marine organisms' skeletal fragments, such as corals and mollusks, and can also contain other minerals like clay and iron carbonate.
